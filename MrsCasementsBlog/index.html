Welcome, Class of 2020! Enjoy Mrs. Casement’s blog!

Welcome, Class of 2020! Mrs. Casement’s Freshmen English classes and writing classes will communicate on this blog site.  A blog is a personal online journal that is intended for others to read.  The online journal will show the reflections and comments of the writers (bloggers).  We will have an exciting online discussion of our literature and all the elements of our classes. Also, we may share our original writing. In fact, we will be  a “community of learners” as we explore our theme, Words Are Powerful.

Readers and writers, please be respectful of each other.  Bloggers, remember to use school appropriate language at all times.  You have wonderful ideas.  Please communicate using the standards of good English.

Internet safety is a real concern for all users.  Therefore, please do not use your full name and email anywhere in your post.  The site automatically identifies you by first initial and last name, and this information is sufficient.

Assignments will be posted with a topic heading and will be labeled by class period.

The rubric for the blogs is on the Library Home Page – Click on  Hot Spots –Blogs.  However, our writing may have longer word counts. Your posts must be approved and will not appear instantly.

Any questions please ask our Library Media Specialist, Mrs. Smolowyk, or me.

Enjoy our blogs for English 9!

PEOPLE WHO CHANGED THE WORLD – Synthesis of our Learning

Congratulations!

Projects, speeches were professional and expert!!

All Classes:

Your projects were interesting, the speeches were informative.   Your writing and speaking is now the work of high school students.  Take a moment to think about how you have grown as a student since September.  Amazing isn’t it!

Now, let’s reflect on the unit of study, People Who Changed the World.  Please think and synthesize your learning and connect with the learning of your classmates.  Learning produces learning.  Write about the experience and your learning when you answer the following nine questions.  Please place the number of the question at the margin and then answer the question.

  1. If it was possible, what advice would the person you studied share with you today?
  2. Did you like/enjoy/admire the person you studied?  Share your thinking.
  3. After listening to the speeches, what person, other than your own, did you find interesting and fascinating?  Explain your thinking.
  4. After listening to the speeches, which person unexpectedly amazed you? (Chose a person other than your answer for #2.) Why?
  5. Examine your notes of the presentations.   Review the qualities each of the people possessed.  –What are the similar qualities these people possessed?  — Did you note any person who had unique qualities?  –Which qualities reviewed in the speeches are the same as those possessed by your person?  –Explain how one acquires these qualities?
  6. From The People Who Changed the World presented in your class, what common characteristics surprised you?  Why do these qualities and characteristics build People Who Change the World?
  7. What is the one element you believe is necessary to be a “World Changer”?  Explain your thinking that brought you to this idea.
  8. Synthesize your learning!  Decide what is the overall knowledge you take away from the unit and share your thinking with us.
  9. How have you become a high school reader and writer?  What can you do to continue to improve your skills and your accomplishments?

All students, THINK…THINK…THINK about the ways you will continue to become the educated people who will create a better world for yourselves and others.  It’s EXCITING!!

Thank you – we enjoyed the unit too.

This is a long blog and will be 300 points.  It is due on or before Wednesday, March 1 at 3:00pm. Please place answers in the comment box.

Keep reading and writing.  Six weeks remain in third quarter.  Spring will be here “before we know it.”

Mrs. Casement and Mrs.Smolowyk

ALL CLASSES – POETRY BLOG – People Who Changed the World

This blog entry is a “poetry blog”.  Write a poem which is a tribute to your Person Who Changed the World.  It should include the person’s name in the title.  Give adjectives that describe the person.  Include a metaphor ( a comparison) or a simile (a comparison using like or as) in your poem. Identify how your subject is a world changer. As always, all writing must be school appropriate.  Do not use texting language. Your poem is submitted in the comment box at the bottom of the page. You may have to click comments and scroll to the bottom to locate the comment box. This blog is worth 200 points and is due by 3:00pm, Wednesday, March 1.  Have fun, poets!!

 Here are three examples:

A Tribute to Marie Curie

I will work

Every day

To forward science,

I will work.

To change the world,

For new discoveries,

I will work.

To advance mankind,

And for my country,

I will work.

For the future, for tomorrow

I will work.

Toying with the unknown,

Or the unknown toying with me,

I will work

Until fatigued and shattered,

My work causes my end.

R. Fischer

[ Note these elements of the poem: The unknown toying with me = a metaphor = radiation –Marie Curie did not know the effects of the radiation in the beginning of her research.

I will work is repeated to show her diligence; fatigued is a metaphor for the end of her life.]

The Need – Inspired by Michelangelo                                                              

These stones shriek out to me,

Testing my craft;

My weary limbs hold staunch.

Oh, they are calling!

Waiting for my hand to droop,

The compulsion is uncontrollable.

I am motivated to learn,

But perhaps from my mistakes.

I shall not relinquish,

For who knows what is to come?

Maybe one day I will change the world.

A. Fowler

[Note:  stones shriek is personification and alliteration in place of a metaphor.

If you have an idea to use an alternative poetry technique, just ask.

Adjectives = weary, uncontrollable, motivated

staunch means loyal or strong]

Will You Join Me, Winston Churchill?

I am a man of fierce ideals,

Will you join me?

I plan to do the impossible;

The only hope for our tiny island home.

My policy is true victory,

So you better not fail.

My enemies strike fear into the hearts of all men

And know nothing of freedom.

So go ahead, join me,

And fight for liberty.

M. Ferrari

[Note:  tiny island home = metaphor for England

Adjectives = fierce, true]

We look forward to reading each other’s poetry!!

GREAT EXPECTATIONS –Blog for Honors –Periods 3 and 5

Charles Dickens once stated, “There is a wisdom of the head, and a wisdom of the heart.”  Analyze how the author conveys this idea in his novel,  GREAT EXPECTATIONS?  How could we apply this idea to our own lives?

You have completed a complex novel, GREAT EXPECTATIONS.  Reflect on your learning.  Share two life lessons you have learned from this novel?  Predict which one of Dickens’ ideas you will be able to apply to your own life of “great expectations”.

Would you classify Dickens as a man who changed his world?  Explain why or why not with at least one piece of evidence.

Select one powerful word or one powerful quote that impressed you during the unit.  Analyze why the word or quote is so powerful.  Recommend the word(s) for others’ thinking.  What did you learn about the need to focus on powerful passages while reading?  How will you read and track meaningful and important quotes during the next book?

Reflect on the process of completing a project.  What have you learned about your independent work style?  If your could create your ideal work environment, what would it be?

Share one element of the unit you enjoyed and share one improvement for next year.

Good luck to all of you as you look forward to your “great expections”!  What wonderful futures you have!

THE PEARL Blog –ALL CLASSES:

Hello Periods 3, 5,8, and 9,

Great job on the projects! I am anxious to see your work as the year progresses.

Kino looked into the pearl and dreamed of many things.   As you dream about your future, what are your plans? What do you hope your future will be?   What are two goals you hope to attain?

As you ponder your future, what life lesson did John Steinbeck show you?  State one other life lesson the novella conveyed.

Did you “see” more in the novella at the end of the unit than you did over the summer? Did you become a better student of literature during the discussions, project and essay? Why/why not? Please share.  Identify one powerful word in Steinbeck’s work or one you chose writing your project.  Why was it powerful.

Next, we will  read another book containing a character who is unhappy and depressed because he wants extreme wealth. In addition, the story will have a character that has extreme wealth but she is unhappy and depressed. Think about the juxtaposition of these two life situations and the characters’ reactions. Think about THE PEARL and “The Necklace”. Can you project what will happen to these two characters?  Are you able to think of a movie that featured unhappy character/s who wanted wealth or character/s who had wealth but were miserable?  What happened? Decide after reading THE PEARL, what Steinbeck would believe to be our “great expectations”.

We have read about and discussed our next author, Charles Dickens.  Identify and state two important aspects of his life.  In addition select two crucial elements of the Victorian times.  State how the four elements you chose are important and crucial. Predict how these four elements will influence Dickens’ writing.  Indeed, it was the “best of times and the worst of times” should make an interesting plot with dynamic characters and thoughtful themes.  Why?

Your blog will be 200 points (approximately 25 minutes of writing).  It is due Friday, Oct. 21 at 3:00 pm. Remember your post will not be published immediately.  Be patient.  It’s fun to see the posts appear.
